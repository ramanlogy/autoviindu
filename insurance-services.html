Insurance & Finance FAQs

Common questions about vehicle insurance and financing in Nepal.

Is third-party insurance mandatory in Nepal?

Yes, third-party insurance is legally required for all vehicles operating on Nepal's roads. Without it, your vehicle cannot legally be on the road and you risk fines at traffic checkpoints.

Do you offer EV-specific insurance coverage?

Yes. We specialize in EV-specific coverage including battery protection, high-voltage system coverage, and the Auto Plus add-on eligibility for used EVs. We assess your vehicle's specific needs and recommend the right insurer.

What documents do I need for vehicle insurance?

Typically: vehicle registration certificate (bluebook), citizenship/ID, previous insurance policy (for renewal), and vehicle photos. For commercial vehicles, additional documentation may be needed.

Can I get an EV loan at a lower interest rate?

Yes. Due to government green loan programs, EV loans from commercial banks typically range from 8.5%–9.5% per annum — lower than ICE vehicle rates. We help you find and apply for these programs.

What is the maximum loan tenure for a new vehicle?

For new ICE vehicles, commercial banks offer up to 7 years. For new EVs, tenure can also extend to 7 years with some lenders offering longer terms due to government EV promotion policies.

How do you assist with insurance claims?

We guide you through the claim filing process, help prepare all required documents, follow up with the insurance company on your behalf, and provide settlement negotiation support to ensure you receive fair compensation.

Can I finance a used vehicle?

Yes, but used vehicle loans carry higher interest rates (11–16%) and lower LTV ratios (40–70%). A mandatory bank valuation is required, and comprehensive insurance is typically compulsory. Age and mileage limits apply per lender.

Do you offer an Insurance + Finance bundle?

Yes. We offer a combined Insurance + Finance service where we handle both your vehicle loan application and insurance policy in one coordinated process, saving you time and often securing better bundled rates.
